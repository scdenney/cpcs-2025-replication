# Dataverse Deposit

All files in this folder are ready to upload to Harvard Dataverse.

## Contents

**Data files**
- `germany_politician.csv` -- German politician conjoint sample (2,071 respondents, 33,136 rows)
- `korea_politician.csv` -- South Korean politician conjoint sample (1,994 respondents, 27,916 rows)
- `nk_politician.csv` -- North Korean migrant politician conjoint sample (311 respondents, 6,220 rows)

**Data dictionaries**
- `data_dictionary_germany.md`
- `data_dictionary_korea.md`
- `data_dictionary_nk.md`

**Code**
- `analysis.R` -- Full analysis script
- `prepare_data.R` -- Data provenance (documents how CSVs were built from raw Qualtrics)
- `run_replication.R` -- One-click replication wrapper

**License**
- `LICENSE` -- CC BY 4.0

## Dataverse metadata

**Title:** Replication Data for: From Division to Democracy: How Political Socialization Shapes Citizen Preferences in Germany and Korea

**Authors:** Nils Brehm (University of Vienna), Angel Hsu Zhou (Leiden University), Steven Denney (Leiden University)

**Description:** Replication data and code for politician conjoint experiments examining how political socialization under divided-nation regimes shapes citizen preferences for emerging politicians in Germany (N=2,071), South Korea (N=1,994), and among North Korean migrants in South Korea (N=311).

**Subject:** Social Sciences

**Keywords:** political socialization, conjoint experiment, divided societies, Germany, South Korea, North Korean migrants, democratic preferences, politician evaluation

**Related publication DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1525/cpcs.2025.2636997
